Types of E-Commerce

  • Business to Consumer (B2C): This is the most common form, where businesses sell products or services directly to consumers. Examples include online retailers like Amazon and eBay.
  • Business to Business (B2B): In this model, businesses sell products or services to other businesses. Examples include wholesale suppliers and manufacturers selling to retailers or other businesses.
  • Consumer to Consumer (C2C): This type involves consumers selling directly to other consumers. Online marketplaces like eBay and Craigslist facilitate these transactions.
  • Consumer to Business (C2B): In this less common model, individuals sell products or offer services to businesses. Examples include freelance platforms like Upwork, where freelancers offer their skills to companies.
  • Business to Government (B2G): This involves businesses providing goods or services to governments or government agencies. Examples include government procurement sites where businesses can bid on government contracts.
  • Government to Business (G2B): Here, the government sells products or services to businesses. This can include things like government data, licenses, or permits.
  • Government to Consumer (G2C): This involves government transactions with individuals. Examples include e-filing taxes and other government services provided online.
